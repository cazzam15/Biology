📋 SQA Key Areas: Cell structure · Transport across membranes · DNA & protein production · Proteins & enzymes · Genetic engineering · Respiration
Section A — Cell Structure & Transport
Q1 · Multiple Choice
Which of the following organelles is found in plant cells BUT NOT in animal cells?
AChloroplast
BMitochondria
CRibosome
DNucleus
✓ A — Chloroplast. Plant cells also have a cell wall (cellulose) and large permanent vacuole — not found in animal cells. Both share mitochondria, ribosomes, nucleus, cell membrane, and cytoplasm.
Q2 · Multiple Choice
A plant cell is placed in a concentrated salt solution. What happens?
ACell bursts (lysis) — too much water enters
BCell swells and becomes turgid
CCell becomes plasmolysed — membrane pulls from wall
DNothing happens — plant cells are unaffected
✓ C — Plasmolysis. Water leaves by osmosis (high water conc. inside → low outside). Vacuole and cytoplasm shrink, membrane pulls away from cell wall. In dilute solution the reverse happens — the cell becomes turgid.
Q3 · Fill in the Blanks — Osmosis Definition
Complete the definition:
Osmosis is the movement of molecules from water concentration to water concentration, through a membrane.
✓ Osmosis is the movement of water molecules from high water concentration to low water concentration, through a selectively permeable membrane.
Q4 · Short Answer (2 marks)
Explain how ACTIVE TRANSPORT differs from diffusion.
✓ Model answer:
• Active transport moves molecules against the concentration gradient (low→high); diffusion moves molecules down the gradient (high→low). (1)
• Active transport requires ATP energy and carrier proteins; diffusion is passive and needs no energy. (1)
Section B — DNA, Proteins & Respiration
Q5 · Multiple Choice
In DNA, which base always pairs with Guanine (G)?
AAdenine (A)
BThymine (T)
CCytosine (C)
DUracil (U)
✓ C — Cytosine. Complementary base pairing: A–T and G–C. This rule always holds in DNA. In RNA, Uracil replaces Thymine (so A pairs with U in RNA).
Q6 · Fill in the Blanks — Protein Synthesis
Complete the sentences about protein synthesis:
A is a section of DNA that codes for one protein.
In the nucleus, is made from DNA by .
This travels to the where builds the protein.
✓ A gene codes for one protein. In the nucleus, mRNA is made by transcription. It travels to the ribosome where translation builds the protein from amino acids.
Q7 · Fill in the Blanks — Respiration
Complete the respiration equations:

Anaerobic (yeast): Glucose → + CO₂

Anaerobic (muscle): Glucose →
✓ Aerobic: Glucose + Oxygen → CO₂ + Water + ATP  |  Yeast: → Ethanol + CO₂  |  Muscle: → Lactic acid
Q8 · Short Answer (5 marks)
List the FIVE stages of genetic engineering used to produce insulin from bacteria, in order.
✓ Five stages (1 mark each):
1. Identify the section of DNA containing the insulin gene on the human chromosome. (1)
2. Extract the insulin gene using restriction enzymes. (1)
3. Extract a plasmid from a bacterial cell. (1)
4. Insert the gene into the plasmid using ligase enzymes. (1)
5. Insert modified plasmid into host bacterium → GMO produces human insulin. (1)

Section A — Nervous System
Q1 · Multiple Choice
What is the correct sequence of structures in a reflex arc?
AReceptor → Sensory neuron → Relay neuron → Motor neuron → Effector
BReceptor → Motor neuron → Relay neuron → Sensory neuron → Effector
CBrain → Sensory neuron → Motor neuron → Effector
DEffector → Relay neuron → Brain → Receptor
✓ A. Receptor (detects stimulus) → Sensory neuron → Relay neuron (spinal cord) → Motor neuron → Effector (muscle/gland). The reflex bypasses conscious brain processing for speed.
Q2 · Short Answer (3 marks)
State the function of each part of the brain: (a) Cerebrum (b) Cerebellum (c) Medulla.
✓ (1 mark each):
(a) Cerebrum: conscious thought, memory, language, voluntary movement. (1)
(b) Cerebellum: balance, coordination, fine motor control. (1)
(c) Medulla: unconscious vital functions — heart rate, breathing rate, blood pressure. (1)

Q5 · Multiple Choice
Which of the following correctly describes XYLEM transport in plants?
ATransports dissolved sugars both up and down the plant
BTransports water and minerals upward from roots to leaves
CTransports oxygen downward to the roots
DTransports water downward from leaves to roots
✓ B. Xylem: dead cells, no end walls, transports water + minerals UPWARD only (transpiration stream). Phloem: living cells, transports dissolved sugars up AND down.

Section A — Ecosystems & Energy
Q1 · Multiple Choice
Which is the correct word equation for PHOTOSYNTHESIS?
AGlucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water + ATP
BCarbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen (light energy)
CGlucose → Ethanol + Carbon dioxide
DWater + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Glucose
✓ B. Photosynthesis: CO₂ + H₂O → Glucose + O₂ (using light energy in chloroplasts). This is the reverse of aerobic respiration and is the foundation of almost all food chains.
Q2 · Short Answer (2 marks)
Construct a food chain using: Oak tree / Caterpillar / Blue tit / Sparrowhawk. Label the producer and primary consumer.
✓ Oak tree → Caterpillar → Blue tit → Sparrowhawk
Producer = Oak tree | Primary consumer = Caterpillar
(Arrows show direction of energy flow — from eaten to eater)
Q3 · Fill in the Blanks — Nitrogen Cycle
bacteria convert N₂ gas into nitrates that plants can absorb.

(bacteria/fungi) break down dead matter, releasing ammonium ions.

bacteria convert nitrates back into N₂ gas, returning it to the atmosphere.
✓ Nitrogen-fixing bacteria → fix N₂. Decomposers → break down dead matter. Denitrifying bacteria → return N₂ to atmosphere.
Section B — Evolution & Natural Selection
Q4 · Multiple Choice
How does antibiotic resistance develop in bacteria? Which explanation is correct?
ABacteria deliberately mutate when they sense antibiotics
BRandom mutations create resistant bacteria; antibiotic use kills non-resistant ones — natural selection
CAntibiotics cause bacteria to produce resistance genes
DBacteria learn to resist antibiotics over their lifetime
✓ B — Natural selection. Random mutations → some bacteria are resistant. Antibiotics kill non-resistant bacteria. Resistant bacteria survive and reproduce → resistance allele becomes more common. This is Darwin's natural selection in action.
Q5 · Extended Answer (5 marks)
Explain the process of natural selection. Include: variation, selection pressure, survival, reproduction, change over generations.
✓ Model answer (1 mark each):
1. Variation — individuals in a population show variation due to random mutations and sexual reproduction. (1)
2. Selection pressure — a challenge (predation, disease, climate) means not all individuals survive equally. (1)
3. Survival — individuals with advantageous traits are more likely to survive ('survival of the fittest'). (1)
4. Reproduction — survivors reproduce and pass on their beneficial alleles to offspring. (1)
5. Change over generations — the advantageous allele becomes more frequent in the population over many generations; over long timescales this can lead to new species (speciation). (1)

🔬 Key Facts & Equations

Essential knowledge for the SQA exam — learn these!

🔬 Cell Biology

Aerobic Respiration
Glucose + Oxygen → CO₂ + Water + ATP
Occurs in: cytoplasm (glycolysis) + mitochondria
Anaerobic — Yeast
Glucose → Ethanol + CO₂ (+ 2 ATP)
Anaerobic — Muscle
Glucose → Lactic acid (+ 2 ATP)
DNA Base Pairing
A — T   |   G — C
Double helix held by complementary base pairs
Osmosis
Water moves: HIGH water conc. → LOW water conc.
Through a selectively permeable membrane (passive)
Protein Synthesis
DNA → mRNA (transcription, in nucleus)
mRNA → Protein (translation, at ribosome)
Genetic Engineering (5 steps)
1. Identify gene → 2. Extract gene (restriction enzymes) → 3. Extract plasmid → 4. Insert gene into plasmid (ligase) → 5. Insert plasmid into host bacterium

🦠 Multicellular Organisms

Reflex Arc
Receptor → Sensory neuron → Relay neuron → Motor neuron → Effector
Blood Glucose Regulation
High glucose → Pancreas releases Insulin → Liver converts glucose→glycogen
Low glucose → Pancreas releases Glucagon → Liver converts glycogen→glucose
Brain Regions
Cerebrum: thought/memory  |  Cerebellum: balance/coordination
Medulla: heart rate/breathing (unconscious)
Genetics Terms
Dominant (capital) = expressed with 1 copy
Recessive (lowercase) = needs 2 copies (homozygous)
Heterozygous = Aa  |  Homozygous = AA or aa
Plant Transport
Xylem: water + minerals UPWARD (dead cells)
Phloem: sugars UP and DOWN (living cells)
Transpiration Rate ↑ when…
↑ Wind speed  |  ↓ Humidity  |  ↑ Temperature  |  ↑ Light intensity

🌍 Life on Earth

Photosynthesis
CO₂ + H₂O → Glucose + O₂ (using light energy, in chloroplasts)
Limiting factors: light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature
Energy Transfer in Food Chains
Only ~10% of energy transfers between trophic levels
~90% lost as heat (respiration, movement, waste)
Nitrogen Cycle
Nitrogen-fixing bacteria: N₂ → nitrates (usable)
Decomposers: dead matter → ammonium
Denitrifying bacteria: nitrates → N₂ (back to atmosphere)
Natural Selection
Variation → Selection pressure → Survival of fittest → Reproduction → Allele frequency changes over generations
Ecological Relationships
Predation: predator benefits, prey harmed
Mutualism: both benefit (e.g. Rhizobium + legumes)
Parasitism: parasite benefits, host harmed
Competition: intraspecific (same sp.) / interspecific (diff. sp.)
